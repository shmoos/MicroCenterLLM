How to Make a Paper Airplane:
1. Take a rectangular piece of paper and fold it in half lengthwise
2. Unfold it and fold the top two corners to meet at the center crease
3. Fold the pointed top down about 2 inches from the tip
4. Fold the top corners to the center again
5. Fold the small triangle up to hold the corners in place
6. Fold the plane in half along the original center crease
7. Create wings by folding each side down at an angle
8. Your paper airplane is ready to fly

The water then collects in oceans, rivers, and lakes, and the cycle continues.

### Cause and Effect

When ice melts, it absorbs heat energy from its surroundings. This is why ice is effective at cooling drinks - it takes thermal energy from the liquid, causing the drink's temperature to decrease while the ice transitions from solid to liquid state.

### Step-by-Step Processes

How to Change a Tire:
1. Find a safe, flat location and turn on hazard lights
2. Apply the parking brake
3. Place wheel wedges behind the tires
4. Remove the hubcap and loosen the lug nuts slightly
5. Use the jack to lift the vehicle off the ground
6. Remove the lug nuts completely and take off the flat tire
7. Mount the spare tire on the hub
8. Hand-tighten the lug nuts
9. Lower the vehicle and remove the jack
10. Fully tighten the lug nuts in a star pattern
11. Check the spare tire's pressure

### Scientific Concepts

Newton's Third Law of Motion states that for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. This means that when one object exerts a force on another object, the second object exerts an equal force in the opposite direction. For example, when you jump, you push down on the ground, and the ground pushes you up with equal force.

The difference between "affect" and "effect":
- "Affect" is typically used as a verb meaning to influence something
- "Effect" is typically used as a noun meaning the result of a change
